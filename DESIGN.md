# Design Constitution

The canonical daily-edition grammar is [skills/personal-newspaper/references/base-design.md](skills/personal-newspaper/references/base-design.md).

The product presents neutral newsprint, dark ink, hairline rules, and deep press red as its only spot color. Songti-style serif carries the newspaper; Kaiti is reserved for the masthead or one accent; sans-serif carries metadata and controls.

Pages use irregular 6–12 column broadsheet composition with one dominant package, differently scaled secondary paths, and distinct adjacent-page silhouettes. Tablets remain multi-column; only phone widths become a single column. Accessibility and article-local interaction are fixed; each day's hierarchy, sections, module spans, imagery, and pacing may change.

The reader shows one content-sized newspaper sheet at a time with page turns on its left and right edges. There is no website navbar or top tab strip, and the paper itself contains no read-more CTA: each complete article package is the accessible opening target, while original sources remain inside the detailed reader.
