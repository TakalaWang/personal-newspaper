# Design Constitution

The canonical daily-edition grammar is [skills/personal-newspaper/references/base-design.md](skills/personal-newspaper/references/base-design.md).

The reader offers three restrained press palettes chosen on first use and changeable at the folio: natural newsprint with dark red spot ink, salmon financial paper with wine ink, and modern white paper with navy spot ink. Generated layout rules consume semantic paper, ink, muted, rule, and spot variables; the trusted reader applies their final values after edition CSS. Songti-style serif carries the newspaper; Kaiti is reserved for the masthead or one accent; sans-serif carries metadata and controls.

Pages use irregular 6–12 column broadsheet composition with one dominant package, differently scaled secondary paths, and distinct adjacent-page silhouettes. Tablets remain multi-column; only phone widths become a single column. Accessibility and article-local interaction are fixed; each day's hierarchy, sections, module spans, imagery, and pacing may change.

Independent vertical lanes and balanced inner columns are preferred when shared grid rows would create cavities. Copy measure determines the shape: a short report shares a lane with another package, while long copy may span or split into readable columns instead of leaving a stretched empty block. When enough verified material exists, neighboring lane endings stay within four body lines; otherwise the thin section is merged instead of padded.

The reader shows one content-sized newspaper sheet at a time. Its top folio states the current and total page count. Full-height left and right edge targets keep faint unboxed chevrons visible without direction text, strengthen them on hover or focus, remain labelled for assistive technology, remain available to touch and keyboard users, and use one restrained paper-turn transition with a reduced-motion alternative. Each article uses one compact final rule that places an optional terminal note beside 喜歡／不喜歡 instead of stacking controls in a separate strip. Sharing creates and copies one edition-scoped link, names active links plainly, supports revocation, and gives the public edition one quiet read-only provenance line without a duplicate masthead. There is no website navbar or top tab strip, and the paper itself contains no read-more CTA: each complete article package is the accessible opening target, while original sources remain inside the detailed reader.
