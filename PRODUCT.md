# Product

## The Personal Daily

A private, one-reader newspaper researched, edited, designed, and published by Codex. The reader knows themselves best; after a short interview and explicit Like / Less like this feedback, Codex becomes the next-best editor of what deserves their attention.

- The reader owns one account and one evolving set of interests. Reading is private by default; the owner may publish a full edition through a non-guessable share capability, with no owner or profile exposure. Revocation immediately denies that capability.
- An initial Codex interview records interests, exclusions, language, timezone, delivery time, and masthead. Delivery time schedules generation in the saved timezone; it does not promise a success notification.
- Codex researches, selects, writes, and assembles each edition; the reader can revisit the archive.
- Reactions inform the next edition only; they do not rewrite a delivered edition.
- The recommendation system is Codex itself: it uses explicit reader context, current public-web research, and evidence quality to decide what deserves space. Core interests are balanced with adjacent and discovery coverage so personalization does not become a filter bubble.
- The product optimizes for informed attention, not engagement feeds or multi-user publishing.
- An edition is a self-contained, readable bundle with its sources and labels preserved.
